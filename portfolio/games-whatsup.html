What's Up?

What’s Up? is a quick one-tap game, adapted from the popular game 'Seven-Up'. With simple controls, it improves your reflexes and test your multiplication skills. Such a game can also be played across all ages, ranging from primary school students to elderlies, bringing joy to everyone in the family.

There are 5 difficulty levels for Single Player mode and 3 multiplayer games, including real-time multiplayer games.

My role was a full stack developer. This project was done in a group of 2 members, as part of NUS Orbital 2017.

Single Player

Single player mode offers 5 difficulty levels (Lame, Easy, Medium, Hard, Extreme). The levels are designed with increasing difficulties and challenges to entice the player as he/she progresses.

How to Play?

  1. Player first chooses which ‘Up’ he would like to play with (or randomly generated, depending on your settings), for example, 7-‘Up’.
  2. When the game begins, the player need to search for the smallest tile in the grid and start tapping the tiles in ascending order. Starting tile is 1.
  3. If the player press the correct tile, the tile will be replaced by a bigger number down the number line, as shown below. Replacement of tile is in random order so the player cannot predict the position of upcoming numbers.
  4. When the player encounter a tile that contains the ‘Up’ digit or is a multiple of the ‘Up’ digit, he avoids it by tapping the Up! button instead. In a 7-‘Up’ game, user need to avoid tiles such as 7,14,17,21,27,28……Failing to press the Up! button will lead to instant Game Over.
  5. If the player press the wrong tile, player loses a life. Pressing wrong tiles for 3 times will lead to Game Over.
  6. Given a time limit of 2 minutes, player will challenge to press as many tiles as possible. Score will be calculated based on the highest number that the player reaches.

Multiplayer

To double the fun, there are 3 multiplayer modes available.

UP & DOWN and hurryUP requires one player to invite another player to join the game. Matching of players is achieved through Firebase Realtime database.

Team-UP can be played by two players simultaneously using when device. It can also be played by one single player.


Modes

  • Hurry UP
    • Match two players.They will play the same game at the same time. Challenge to see who reaches the target first.
  • UP & DOWN
    • Match two players. This mode has no time limit. For the first round, player A starts from increasing numbers (1,2,3...) while player B starts from decreasing numbers (100,99, 98...). Depending on where they meet, the players will get corresponding scores. They then switch position (player A starts from decreasing numbers and player B starts from increasing numbers). Combine scores from two rounds of game and determine winner.
  • Team UP
    • Time limit is imposed. The screen will display a UP buttons on both ends of the screen. Each player is in charge of half the grid and one UP button. Which UP button is to be pressed depends on the location of the UP number, for example, if the UP number appears on lower half of the screen, then the player in charge of the lower UP button must press it.
